## AI Response

Beautiful sermon image — those early believers literally roped themselves together so the storm couldn't scatter them. The rope was a confession: *if you go under, I go with you.* Here are 5 questions to sit with:

---

**1. What rope are you currently holding?**
When the storm hits this week — the diagnosis, the layoff, the wayward kid, the marriage strain — *who* is the person (or community) you're roped to? Not who you *follow* online. Who would notice within 24 hours if you went silent?

**2. What are you tied to that *isn't* a rope — it's an anchor dragging you down?**
The early Christians chose the rope. We often get tied to things we never consented to: a grudge, a screen, an income bracket, a reputation, an old identity God already buried. **Hebrews 12:1** — "let us lay aside every weight, and the sin which doth so easily beset us." What needs cutting before the next wave?

**3. If the storm took everything but the rope, would what's on the other end still be worth surviving for?**
The early church wasn't tied to a *building* or a *brand* — they were tied to *each other in Christ*. Strip away the music, the lights, the programs, the platform. **Is the Person at the other end of your rope still Jesus?** Or have you quietly tied yourself to the *experience* of church instead of the *Lord* of the church?

**4. Whose rope are *you*?**
Discipleship isn't just being held — it's being the hand that holds. Paul to the Thessalonians: *"we were willing to have imparted unto you, not the gospel of God only, but also our own souls"* (1 Thess 2:8). Name one person right now whose rope you are. If you can't, that's the next assignment — not a new Bible study. A rope.

**5. When the storm passes, will the rope have made you *stronger together* — or will you cut it the moment the sun comes out?**
The early Christians stayed roped after the storm. That's why the church survived Nero, Diocletian, the lions. Storms reveal the rope; *calm tests whether you keep it.* Are you a fair-weather believer with fair-weather friends — or roped for the long voyage home?

---

> *"Bear ye one another's burdens, and so fulfil the law of Christ."* — **Galatians 6:2**

The rope isn't a metaphor. It's the law of Christ. 🪢
