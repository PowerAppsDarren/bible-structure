# Exchange 04: "What problems have you written off as too small for God?"

- **Timestamp:** 2026-05-17
- **Context:** Sermon reflection prompt. Model bridged it back to Jonah 4 (the plant, the worm, the cattle — God's attention to small things).

## User Input

> what problems have you written off as too small for God's attention?

## AI Response

Oof. That's a Jonah 4 question if there ever was one.

Remember how Jonah's story *ends*? Not with the revival. Not with the king in sackcloth. It ends with God saying:

> *"And should I not pity Nineveh... and **also much cattle**?"* (Jonah 4:11)

**God's last recorded word in the book is about cows.**

The God who runs galaxies counted the livestock. The God who topples empires noticed the worm (4:7), appointed the plant (4:6), prepared the east wind (4:8). Every "small" detail in Jonah 4 is something *God Himself arranged*. Nothing was beneath His attention.

So let's name them — the things we've quietly filed under "too small to bother God with":

---

🪴 **The plant-sized things.**
The headache that won't quit. The car making *that* noise. The awkward conversation you have to have Tuesday. The lost keys. The kid's math homework. The recipe that keeps flopping. *"God's got bigger things to deal with."* Does He? **Matthew 10:30** — *"the very hairs of your head are all numbered."* He counts hair follicles. He's not too busy for your Tuesday.

🐛 **The worm-sized things.**
The small resentment you've decided isn't worth confessing. The little compromise nobody sees. The quiet jealousy. The 30-second scroll that became 90 minutes. *"It's not a real sin."* But it's eating something. The worm in Jonah 4:7 destroyed the whole plant overnight.

🌬️ **The east-wind things — the chronic irritations.**
The coworker who grates. The bodily pain you've stopped praying about because it never changes. The financial squeeze that's just always *there*. The loneliness that's become background noise. *"God already knows. No point asking again."* But Luke 18 — the persistent widow — Jesus told that parable *so that men ought always to pray, and not to faint.*

🐄 **The "much cattle" things — other people's small problems.**
The neighbor's grief you don't have words for. The cashier who looked tired. The cousin you haven't called. The single mom three pews back. *"That's not my problem."* It's God's. And He's looking for someone to be His hands and feet about it.

🌱 **The seed things — the dreams you've shrunk.**
The book idea. The ministry nudge. The reconciliation you've stopped hoping for. The prayer you used to pray and quit. *"I don't want to bother Him with something so personal."* But **Zechariah 4:10** — *"Who hath despised the day of small things?"* The God who values mustard seeds (Matt 13:31), widow's mites (Luke 21:2), and cups of cold water (Matt 10:42) doesn't grade prayers by importance.

---

## The pattern underneath all of it

When we say *"too small for God,"* what we usually mean is one of three things:

1. **"I don't really believe He cares about me specifically."** (A theology problem — fixed by 1 Peter 5:7)
2. **"I want to handle it myself."** (A pride problem — fixed by repentance)
3. **"I've been disappointed before, so I've stopped asking."** (A wound problem — fixed by being honest with Him about *that*)

Jonah's God appointed a fish, a plant, a worm, a wind — and chased down one bitter prophet sitting under a dead vine just to ask him a question. **Nothing about you is too small.** The fact that *you* are tracking it means *He* is tracking it.

> *"Casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you."* — **1 Peter 5:7**

The Greek there — **μέριμνα (merimna)** — is the word for *every anxiety, every distraction, every dividing concern, large or small.* Not the big ones. *All* of them.

Bring Him the cattle. 🐄
