# 📖 John 10:7–17 — "Life. And Then Some."
### A Deep Devotional Study

The sermon just walked you to the verse the whole Bible is pointing at:

> *"I am come that they might have life, and that they might have it more abundantly."* (John 10:10)

Let's go all the way in.

---

## 🐺 Verses 8 & 10a — The Thief's Job Description

> *"All that ever came before me are thieves and robbers... The thief cometh not, but for to steal, and to kill, and to destroy."* (10:8, 10a)

Three Greek verbs. Memorize them. They are the **enemy's entire playbook** — and they don't change.

- **κλέψῃ (klepsē)** — to *steal* (root: *kleptō* → kleptomaniac). To take what isn't his.
- **θύσῃ (thysē)** — to *kill, slaughter, sacrifice.* Same root used for slaughtering animals on a pagan altar.
- **ἀπολέσῃ (apolesē)** — to *destroy, ruin, render utterly lost.* The root of *Apollyon* — the destroyer of Revelation 9:11.

That's the spec sheet. The enemy never just steals. He steals so he can kill. He kills so he can destroy. **Theft → death → ruin.** It's a pipeline, not a buffet.

- He steals your joy → so he can kill your prayer life → so he can destroy your witness.
- He steals your marriage's tenderness → so he can kill the friendship → so he can destroy the home.
- He steals your kid's attention → so he can kill their wonder → so he can destroy their faith.

**Every "small" theft is a foothold for the next two verbs.** This is why Jesus uses *all three* — He's exposing the whole supply chain.

And right against that — **"I AM COME that they might have *life*."** Not three verbs. **One.** Because His one verb undoes all three of His enemy's.

---

## ❄️ THE VERSE — *"Life, and Have It More Abundantly"*

> *"I am come that they might have life, and that they might have it more abundantly."* (John 10:10b)

This is the most undersold verse in the New Testament. Let's open the Greek bottle:

**ἵνα ζωὴν ἔχωσιν καὶ περισσὸν ἔχωσιν**
*"...in order that they might have life — and have *perisson*."*

### *ζωή (zōē)* — Not Just Heartbeat

Greek has two words for "life":
- **βίος (bios)** — physical existence, what biology measures. The duration of your heartbeat.
- **ζωή (zōē)** — *the life that comes from God Himself.* Divine life. Eternal-quality life. The life Jesus shares with the Father from before the foundation of the world.

When Jesus says *"I am come that they might have life,"* He doesn't mean *"more years on Earth."* He means **the very life of God transplanted into a human soul.** John 1:4 — *"in him was life (zōē), and the life was the light of men."* That zōē. The uncreated kind.

### *περισσόν (perisson)* — More Than the Container Can Hold

Then He adds **περισσόν** — translated *"more abundantly,"* but the word means *exceeding, beyond measure, overflowing, super-added, more than enough.*

Paul uses the same root in **Ephesians 3:20** — *"Now unto him that is able to do exceeding abundantly above (huper-ek-perissou) all that we ask or think..."* — *over-and-above-overflowing-superabundantly.* Paul has to stack three prefixes on perisson to translate what Jesus says here in a single word.

**Picture a glass under a waterfall.** The glass is *zōē* — divine life poured in until it's full. *Perisson* is what happens *after* the glass is full. **The waterfall doesn't turn off.** It overflows the rim, soaks the table, drenches the floor, fills the room.

Jesus doesn't come to **fill you.** He comes to **make you spill.**

This is why Christianity that stays inside a Sunday morning was never the design. The waterfall isn't impressed with your container. It's looking for the *overflow* — the marriage made beautiful, the workplace where you carry a different atmosphere, the friend who gets prayed for at 2 AM, the stranger you can't unsee.

> *"Out of his belly shall flow rivers of living water."* — **John 7:38**

Same Jesus. Same overflow. The river doesn't stay in you. **You become a tributary.**

## 🌟 Verse 11 — *"I Am the Good Shepherd"*

> *"I am the good shepherd: the good shepherd giveth his life for the sheep."* (10:11)

Eighth I AM statement (depending on count). Stop and breathe on the Greek word for "good":

**ὁ ποιμὴν ὁ καλός (ho poimēn ho kalos)** — *the shepherd, the beautiful one.*

Greek has two words for "good":
- **ἀγαθός (agathos)** — morally good, ethically right
- **καλός (kalos)** — *beautiful, noble, ideal, attractive, the kind of good you want to look at, the good that draws you in*

Jesus chooses **kalos.** Not *"I'm the morally upright shepherd."* But *"I'm the gorgeous shepherd. The ideal one. The one the entire concept 'shepherd' has been pointing toward."*

He's the **template** every shepherd in Scripture was a sketch of:
- Abel (Gen 4) — the first shepherd, murdered for his offering
- Jacob (Gen 30) — the shepherd whose flock multiplied supernaturally
- Joseph (Gen 37) — the shepherd-son rejected by his brothers
- Moses (Ex 3) — 40 years a shepherd before delivering Israel
- David (1 Sam 17) — the shepherd-king
- *Every one of them was a chapter of a story whose **kalos** was Jesus.*

Dr. Bill Creasy reads this as the *climax of the biblical shepherd-motif* — every shepherd from Abel to David is a watercolor; Jesus is the oil painting they were studies for.

## 💸 Verses 12–13 — The Hireling

> *"But he that is an hireling, and not the shepherd, whose own the sheep are not, seeth the wolf coming, and leaveth the sheep, and fleeth: and the wolf catcheth them, and scattereth the sheep. The hireling fleeth, because he is an hireling, and careth not for the sheep."* (10:12-13)

The Greek **μισθωτός (misthōtos)** — "hireling" — from *misthos* = wages. The man who shepherds **for the paycheck.**

This isn't subtle. Jesus is firing a missile at the religious leadership of His day. Read it against **Ezekiel 34** — *"Woe to the shepherds of Israel that do feed themselves! should not the shepherds feed the flocks?... With force and with cruelty have ye ruled them. And they were scattered, because there is no shepherd."* (Ez 34:2, 4-5)

Six hundred years later, Jesus walks in and says: **"That was you. And I am the answer God promised back then."** Because Ezekiel 34:11-15 promised: *"For thus saith the Lord GOD; Behold, I, even I, will both search my sheep, and seek them out... I will feed my flock, and I will cause them to lie down, saith the Lord GOD."*

**Jesus is YHWH-the-Shepherd of Ezekiel 34, in skin.** That's the claim. The Pharisees caught it (10:31 — they pick up stones).

## 👁️ Verses 14–15 — The Mutual Knowing

> *"I am the good shepherd, and know my sheep, and am known of mine. As the Father knoweth me, even so know I the Father: and I lay down my life for the sheep."* (10:14-15)

The Greek verb **γινώσκω (ginōskō)** — to know **experientially, relationally, intimately.** Not the *oida* knowledge of facts. The knowledge of relationship. The same word the Septuagint uses in Genesis 4:1 — *"And Adam knew Eve his wife."* Covenant-deep knowing.

Now read the staggering parallel structure:

> *I know my sheep — as the Father knows me.*
> *My sheep know me — as I know the Father.*

**Jesus is saying that the knowing between Him and you is patterned on the eternal knowing between the Father and the Son.** The intimacy you have access to as a sheep is *the same intimacy that has existed inside the Trinity from eternity past.* You are not on the outside of God's inner life looking in. **You have been drawn into the conversation.**

This is what John Bevere calls *the fear of the Lord that births intimacy* — when you realize Who is knowing you and the kind of knowing it is. Not surveillance. **Covenant.**

---

## 🌍 Verse 16 — The Other Sheep

> *"And other sheep I have, which are not of this fold: them also I must bring, and they shall hear my voice; and there shall be one fold, and one shepherd."* (10:16)

Jesus tells a 1st-century Jewish audience: **I have other sheep.** Outside this fold. *They* will hear my voice too. *They* will come.

He's announcing the **Gentile mission** seven chapters before Acts 1. He's announcing the **global church** before the cross. Every nation, every language, every tribe — *sheep He already owns* and is going to call.

Chuck Missler loved to point out: **the Greek says "must" — δεῖ (dei)** — divine necessity. Not "I'd like to bring them." *"I MUST."* The Gentile inclusion isn't Plan B after Israel rejected Him. **It was always Plan A.** Genesis 12:3 — *"in thee shall all families of the earth be blessed."*

You — if you're not ethnically Jewish — are an **"other sheep"** that Jesus owned **before John 10 was preached.** He pointed at you before you were born. *"That one over there. She's mine too. I'm coming for her."*

## ✋ Verses 17–18 — Nobody Takes It

> *"Therefore doth my Father love me, because I lay down my life, that I might take it again. No man taketh it from me, but I lay it down of myself. I have power to lay it down, and I have power to take it again. This commandment have I received of my Father."* (10:17-18)

Four times in two verses: **"I lay it down."** Same verb each time — **τίθημι (tithēmi)** — *to deliberately set down, to place, to position with purpose.*

This is **the most important sentence in the theology of the cross.** Jesus is saying:

> *"The Romans don't take My life. The Sanhedrin doesn't take My life. Judas doesn't take My life. Pilate doesn't take My life. **I lay it down.** And then I pick it up again — by My own authority — because My Father told Me to."*

The cross was not a tragedy that overtook Jesus. **It was a mission He carried out.** He wasn't a victim of the empire. **He was the Lamb who walked into the slaughterhouse with the key to the back door in His pocket.**

And the resurrection wasn't God reacting to a setback. **It was Jesus exercising authority He had the whole time.** *"I have power to take it again."* The Greek **ἐξουσίαν (exousian)** — *legal authority, jurisdictional right.* He has the *right* to come back. He had it on Friday. He used it on Sunday.

This is why Easter is not a hope. **It's a paycheck.** A receipt for work completed exactly as scheduled.

## 🐍 The Spiritual Warfare Layer — Why John 10:10 Is Quoted Wrong

Notice how this verse is usually preached:

> *"The thief comes to steal, kill, and destroy — but I have come that they might have life and have it more abundantly!"*

We **skip the "but"** part of how the verse actually splits. Read it again:

> *"The thief cometh not, but for to steal, and to kill, and to destroy: I am come that they might have life..."*

There's **no "but."** Just a colon. Just two side-by-side facts: *the thief does this. I came to do this.*

Why does that matter? Because the *"but I have come"* reading makes Jesus sound like He showed up *in response to* the thief. As if Satan moved first and God reacted. **He didn't.** Jesus' coming was decided in eternity past (1 Peter 1:20 — *"foreordained before the foundation of the world"*). The thief is the reactionary. **Jesus is the eternal initiative.**

This is the deepest level of comfort in John 10. Your salvation **was not Plan B.** Your rescue was not improvised. The Shepherd didn't get woken up at 3 AM by news of a wolf. **He set the schedule for the wolf, the cross, the resurrection, the empty tomb, and your bedtime tonight.**

Ephesians 6:12 reminds you that the wolves are real — *principalities, powers, rulers of the darkness of this age.* But they are **fenced wolves.** The fold has walls and the gate has a Shepherd. **They can pace. They cannot enter.**

---

## 🕊️ Closing Reflection — What "Abundant" Actually Means

Some Christians read *"life more abundantly"* and hear **prosperity gospel** — money, comfort, success. That's not what Jesus said. *Perisson* is the *overflow of divine zōē* — and divine life can overflow in a hospital bed, a prison cell, a war zone, a depression, a bankruptcy.

Paul wrote about *perisson* from a Roman dungeon (Phil 4:11-13). Stephen experienced it under a rain of stones (Acts 7:55). The Hebrews 11 saints lived it while being *"destitute, afflicted, tormented; (of whom the world was not worthy)"* (Heb 11:37-38).

The abundance is not in the *circumstances.* It is in the **Shepherd inside the circumstances.**

If your life right now looks more like Lamentations 3:1-18 than John 10:10b — the Shepherd is *still* there. The Door is *still* open. The waterfall is *still* falling. **Your container just got tipped a different direction.**

Hold the cup back under the falls.
