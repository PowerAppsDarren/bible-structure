# 🌅 "All Day Long" — The Hidden Mercy of Lamentations 3 → John 10

The preacher just made the move that unlocks the whole book. Watch:

---

## 🔁 The Inversion You Almost Missed

Go back to **Lamentations 3:3** —
> *"Surely against me is he turned; he turneth his hand against me **all the day**."*

That's Jeremiah at the bottom. The hand of God against him *all day*.

Now jump down to **Lamentations 3:25** —
> *"The LORD is good unto them that wait for him, to the soul that seeketh him."*

And the next verse —
> *"It is good that a man should both hope and quietly wait for the salvation of the LORD."* (3:26)

The Hebrew word for "good" in v. 25 — **טוֹב (tov)** — is the *same word* God spoke over creation in Genesis 1: *"and God saw that it was tov."* Original-creation goodness. Uncorrupted goodness. **The kind of good that was good before sin ever showed up.**

Here's the inversion the Holy Spirit hides in the structure:
- v. 3 — *His hand against me all the day*
- v. 25 — *He is good to those who wait for Him*

**The same "all day" that felt like wrath was actually faithfulness.** Jeremiah couldn't see it at v. 3. By v. 25, he can. **The hand didn't change. The eyes did.**

This is what suffering does in the saint — *eventually*. It re-reads the autobiography. The seasons you thought were God against you turn out to be God *for* you in a costume your nervous system couldn't decode at the time. Romans 8:28 isn't a slogan; it's a **retrospective discovery**.

## 💔 The Sermon's Knife — "What If God Had the Level of Patience We Have for Ourselves?"

Sit with that for a second. *Really* sit.

How patient are you with yourself?

- You fall in the same sin **three times this month** and start whispering *"maybe I'm not actually saved."*
- You miss devotions **for a week** and feel like you have to crawl back to God.
- You can't shake the anxiety after **two sessions of prayer** and conclude God isn't listening.
- You've been waiting on a breakthrough **eight months** and you're already drafting the eulogy for the dream.

**If God had your patience with yourself, He would have written you off in 2009.**

But look at the Hebrew of Lamentations 3:22 — *"It is of the LORD'S mercies that we are not consumed, because his compassions fail not."*

- "Mercies" — **חַסְדֵי יְהוָה (chasdei YHWH)** — *the hesed-loves of the LORD.* **Covenant loyalty that does not quit.**
- "Fail not" — **לֹא־תָמְנוּ (lo-tamnu)** — *"are not finished, are not used up, are not exhausted."*

God's mercies have a **vessel that does not bottom out.** Yours? *You ran out of self-mercy by Tuesday.* His? **He's been merciful "all day long" to people who haven't even noticed yet.**

> *"The LORD is longsuffering, and of great mercy..."* — **Numbers 14:18**

The Hebrew **אֶרֶךְ אַפַּיִם (erech appayim)** — literally *"long of nostrils."* Slow to *snort with anger*. God's nose is long. Ours is short. He breathes in patience for the time it takes a soul to wake up. We exhale judgment after one inconvenience.

**The "all day long" of His goodness is precisely the "all day long" you experienced as His silence.** Same day. Same hand. Different reading.

## 🚪 The Gate That Was Always There — John 10:7

Now the preacher pivots — and this is *masterful*.

(Quick note — *"I am the gate for the sheep"* is **John 10:7** in modern translations; **v. 17** is the gorgeous *"Therefore doth my Father love me, because I lay down my life, that I might take it again."* But hold both — because they're saying the same thing two different ways.)

> *"Then said Jesus unto them again, Verily, verily, I say unto you, **I am the door of the sheep**."* (John 10:7)

The Greek — **Ἐγώ εἰμι ἡ θύρα τῶν προβάτων** — *"I myself am the door of the sheep."*

**Ἐγώ εἰμι (egō eimi)** — *I AM.* This is the **seventh "I AM" statement** in John's gospel (depending on how you count) — a direct echo of **Exodus 3:14**, the divine Name. Jesus isn't saying *"I show you the way to the gate."* He's saying *"I AM the gate. The God who said I AM at the burning bush now says I AM at the sheepfold."*

### What Was an Ancient Sheepfold?

This is the part Chuck Missler loved to highlight. In Israelite shepherding, a sheepfold was a **stone enclosure with one opening — no door, just a gap.** At night, the shepherd would **lay his body across the opening.** Anything that wanted in or out — predator or sheep — had to **cross over the shepherd's body.** The shepherd's body *was* the door.

So when Jesus says **"I am the gate"** in v. 7, and then **"I lay down my life for the sheep"** in v. 15, and then **"I lay down my life that I might take it again"** in v. 17 — *He's describing one act.* **He becomes the door by laying His body across the only opening between His sheep and everything that wants to devour them.**

## 🧱 Lamentations 3 + John 10 — The Sermon's Synthesis

Now read the two passages together. This is what the preacher is doing — and it is **brilliant**:

| **Lamentations 3:7-9** | **John 10:7** |
|---|---|
| *"He hath hedged me about, that I cannot get out: he hath made my chain heavy."* | *"I am the gate for the sheep."* |
| *"When I cry and shout, he shutteth out my prayer."* | *"By me if any man enter in, he shall be saved, and shall go in and out, and find pasture."* (v. 9) |
| *"He hath inclosed my ways with hewn stone."* | *"I am come that they might have life, and that they might have it more abundantly."* (v. 10) |

**The "hedge" of Lamentations 3:7 turns out to be the sheepfold of John 10.**

Jeremiah felt *trapped*. **He was actually being kept.** The walls he experienced as a prison were the walls of a **fold**, and the One he experienced as silent at the gate **had not yet revealed Himself as the gate.**

But when Jesus shows up in John 10 and says **"I AM the gate"** — He's the answer to every "hedged me about" in Lamentations 3. **The hedge was love. The wall was protection. The silence was a Shepherd lying across the only opening so nothing — nothing — could get to His sheep without going through His body first.**

> *"Neither shall any man pluck them out of my hand."* — **John 10:28**

That's not poetry. That's the *door of the sheepfold* speaking. The thief comes (10:10) — *and meets the body of the Shepherd at the entrance.* Sin, death, the accuser, the diagnosis, the bankruptcy, the betrayal — **every wolf in your life had to cross over the body of Jesus to get to you.** And it didn't.

## ❄️ The Verse You Should Write in the Margin

> *"Therefore doth my Father love me, because **I lay down my life, that I might take it again**."* — **John 10:17**

The Greek — **τίθημι τὴν ψυχήν μου, ἵνα πάλιν λάβω αὐτήν** — *"I place my soul down, in order that I might receive it back again."*

That word **τίθημι (tithēmi)** — to *deliberately set in place.* Not *"I let it be taken."* **"I place it. I lay it down on purpose. I put it exactly where it needs to be — across the gate."**

This is the answer to *"what if God had your patience with yourself?"*

**He doesn't.** Because the Shepherd who laid Himself down at the gate **had to choose to lay down again every morning of those years you were wandering.** The patience that did not run out *cost Him His life.* And He **took it back up** specifically so that "all day long" His goodness could keep meeting you — even when you'd given up on yourself.

> *"For as the heavens are high above the earth, so great is his mercy toward them that fear him."* — **Psalm 103:11**

The Greek of John 10:17 uses **ἀγαπᾷ (agapa)** — *the Father loves the Son* in the present continuous tense. The Father is *right now, continually* loving the Son **because** the Son laid Himself down as the gate. Which means: every time you walk *through* Jesus — every prayer, every confession, every return — **the Father's love for the Son is being re-ratified over your head.**

You can't out-wander His patience. You can't out-fail His "all day long." You can't out-stubborn the Shepherd at the door.

## 🕊️ The Take-Home

Three things to journal on the back of your bulletin:

**1. The "all day long" you remember as wrath might have been faithfulness in disguise.**
Re-read your hardest year through Lamentations 3:25. *Same day. Same hand. Different eyes.*

**2. Extend to yourself the patience God extends to you.**
Not so you can sin freely — but so you can *get back up freely.* The enemy doesn't mind if you fall; he just wants you to stay down. **God's patience exists to fund your rising.**

**3. Walk through the Gate today.**
You don't have to figure out the maze. You don't have to climb the wall (John 10:1 — *that's what the thief does*). **Enter through the Door.** His body is already laid down across the only opening. Just walk through.

> *"I am the door: by me if any man enter in, he shall be saved, and shall go in and out, and find pasture."* — **John 10:9**

**Saved. Go in. Go out. Find pasture.** Four gifts at one Gate.
